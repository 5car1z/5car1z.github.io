Magical Technology

Through the wonders of modern technology it is now possible to access an unfathomable amount of resources over todays interwebs. Not all of which are as recent as the tech supporting it.

The Open Knowledge Foundation’s Public Domain Review website house all manner of olden day film, audio, book, and image content. This vast array of digital materials are freely available for anyones viewing pleasure (or displeasure as the case might be).

Below are some images taken directly from an electronically archived victorian book, covering magic, illusion, trick photography, and the aptly named “scientific diversions”.

The book itself is titled:

Magic: stage illusions and scientific diversions, including trick photography, compiled and edited by Albert A. Hopkins, with an introduction by Henry Ridgely Evans; 1897; Low, London.

And can be viewed in its entirety here on their website:

http://archive.org/stream/magicstageillusi00hopk#page/n7/mode/2up

Or you can even download the book in a variety of formats: PDF, EPUB, MOBI, there’s a torrent link provided too.

I should also add that this organisation (like many of this type) rely on independant funding and donations from randomers like me and you. Here’s the link and details on how to help out if you so desire to:

http://publicdomainreview.org/support/

And yes I will be donating!

I hope you are as entertained by the surreal illustrations as much as I am, and can see the potential long term value of projects such as this.
